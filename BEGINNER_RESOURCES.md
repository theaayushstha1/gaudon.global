## 2. Types of Silicone Sealants

### By Cure Chemistry

| Type | Cure Method | Byproduct | Best For |
|------|-------------|-----------|----------|
| **Acetoxy** | Moisture cure | Acetic acid (vinegar smell) | Glass, ceramics, non-porous surfaces |
| **Neutral Cure** | Moisture cure | Alcohol/Oxime | Metals, concrete, sensitive surfaces |
| **Alkoxy** | Moisture cure | Alcohol | General purpose, low odor |
| **Oxime** | Moisture cure | Ketoxime | Building/construction |

### By Application Type

**1. General Purpose Silicone**
- Basic sealing needs
- Indoor/outdoor use
- Price point: Low-Medium

**2. Structural Silicone**
- High-rise curtain wall glazing
- Requires certification
- Price point: Premium

**3. Weatherproofing Silicone**
- Exterior joints
- UV and weather resistant
- Price point: Medium-High

**4. Sanitary/Kitchen & Bath**
- Mold and mildew resistant
- Food-safe options available
- Price point: Medium

**5. High-Temperature Silicone**
- Automotive, industrial
- Withstands 250-315°C
- Price point: High

**6. Specialty Silicone**
- Fire-rated
- Low VOC
- Food grade
- Price point: Premium

**Steps:**
1. Document quality management processes
2. Hire certification consultant
3. Internal audit preparation
4. Certification body audit

## 8. Essential Industry Knowledge

### Key Performance Properties

**What Customers Care About:**

| Property | Why It Matters | How to Measure |
|----------|----------------|----------------|
| **Adhesion** | Bonding strength | Peel/shear tests (ASTM) |
| **Flexibility** | Joint movement | Modulus rating |
| **Cure Time** | Time to usable bond | Tack-free time, full cure |
| **Temperature Range** | Operating conditions | -60°C to +250°C typical |
| **UV Resistance** | Outdoor durability | Accelerated weathering |
| **Mold Resistance** | Wet area performance | ASTM G21 testing |

### Understanding Specifications

**Common Spec Sheet Terms:**
- **Skin Time**: Time before sealant forms surface skin
- **Tack-Free Time**: When sealant is no longer sticky
- **Full Cure**: Complete cross-linking (typically 7-21 days)
- **Shore A Hardness**: Material flexibility (lower = softer)
- **Elongation at Break**: Stretch before failure
- **Movement Capability**: Joint expansion/compression (%)

### Color Options

Standard colors to offer:
- Clear/Transparent
- White
- Black
- Gray/Aluminum
- Bronze
- Brown
- Tan/Beige

Premium: Custom color matching

## 11. Glossary of Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Adhesion** | Bond strength between sealant and substrate |
| **Cohesion** | Internal strength of sealant material |
| **Cure** | Chemical process of sealant hardening |
| **Elastomer** | Rubber-like polymer with elastic properties |
| **Extrusion** | Pushing sealant through a nozzle |
| **Joint** | Gap or space to be sealed |
| **Modulus** | Measure of stiffness/flexibility |
| **Primer** | Surface preparation chemical |
| **RTV** | Room Temperature Vulcanizing |
| **Substrate** | Surface being sealed |
| **Tack** | Stickiness of uncured sealant |
| **VOC** | Volatile Organic Compounds |
| **Weathering** | Degradation from weather exposure |
